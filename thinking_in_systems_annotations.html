Thinking in Systems

by Donella H. Meadows

Raw highlights, quotes, and notes captured while reading.

Read Feb'26 - May'26

Introduction: The Systems Lens

  • As our world continues to change rapidly and become more complex, systems thinking will help us manage, adapt, and see the wide range of choices we have before us.

Part One: System Structure and Behavior | One. The Basics

  • The best way to deduce the system’s purpose is to watch for a while to see how the system behaves.
  • A change in purpose changes a system profoundly, even if every element and interconnection remains the same.
  • All are essential. All interact. All have their roles. But the least obvious part of the system, its function or purpose, is often the most crucial determinant of the system’s behavior.

Part Two: Systems and Us | Three. Why Systems Work So Well

  • When systems work well, we see a kind of harmony in their functioning.
  • Resilience is a measure of a system’s ability to survive and persist within a variable environment. The opposite of resilience is brittleness or rigidity.

Seven. Living in a World of Systems

  • Before you disturb the system in any way, watch how it behaves.
  • Remember that hierarchies exist to serve the bottom layers, not the top.
  • The thing to do, when you don’t know, is not to bluff and not to freeze, but to learn.
  • What’s appropriate when you’re learning is small steps, constant monitoring, and a willingness to change course as you find out more about where it’s leading.
